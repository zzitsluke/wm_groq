Tips & Strategies by Study Mode
📋
Study Notes
Best for: first pass on a new module
Use this before anything else on a new module — it orients you to the key frameworks before you drill them.
After reading, immediately switch to Quiz without re-reading — this reveals what actually stuck vs. what felt familiar.
Use the Pin button to save your notes — they'll be there when you return, no re-generation needed.
The Common Exam Themes section is written specifically for test prep — read those across all modules the night before an exam.
Use Print → Save as PDF for offline review or to annotate on your tablet.
🃏
Flashcards
Best for: active recall after first read
Be honest with the ratings — only mark Known if you could explain it out loud without the card in front of you.
After finishing, click Review Marked Cards to drill only the ones you flagged — don't skip this step.
Use Shuffle after your first pass — it breaks pattern recognition and forces genuine recall.
Hit Regenerate for a fresh set of 12 cards on the same module — the AI never gives you identical cards twice.
Combine with Quiz: run flashcards first, then take the quiz cold — your score difference tells you exactly where the gaps are.
🧠
Interactive Quiz
Best for: identifying gaps after studying
Take it cold first — don't re-read your notes beforehand. A lower score now is more useful than a false 80% later.
After finishing, use Review Missed — then immediately click Ask Tutor about this concept on each wrong answer.
Score below 60%? Go back to Study Notes or Flashcards first, then retake — don't just guess through it again.
Retake the same module quiz after studying — track your improvement in Session Stats over time.
One question is always an application question — practice applying the concept to a new scenario, not just recalling definitions.
🎓
Tutor Chat
Best for: filling gaps, going deeper
Ask it to simplify: "Explain [concept] like I've never heard of it" forces the AI to strip away jargon.
Ask for real examples: "Give me 3 companies that successfully applied this framework and 2 that failed — and explain why."
Challenge your understanding: "What's the most common mistake students make when applying [concept]?"
Compare and contrast: "What's the difference between Blue Ocean Strategy and competitive positioning?" works better than broad questions.
Generate practice questions: "Give me a hard exam question on this topic, then tell me what a strong answer would include."
Apply to your interests: "How would a healthcare startup apply this framework differently than a Fortune 500 retailer?"
🏢
Case Study
Best for: applying concepts to real scenarios
Be specific with your industry — "B2B SaaS startup" generates a far richer case than just "Tech." The more specific, the better.
Use Executive difficulty for exam prep and MBA-level for regular study — the decision complexity scales significantly.
Write your answer first before hitting Get Feedback — don't scroll to the AI response without attempting it. You'll retain far more.
Time yourself on the discussion questions — 8-10 minutes per question simulates real exam and case interview conditions.
Try the same module with 3 different industries — healthcare vs. retail vs. tech shows you how the framework flexes in different contexts.
📝
Exam Prep
Best for: final prep, simulating test conditions
Always select at least 3–4 modules — single-module exams don't reflect how cross-topic questions appear on real tests.
Always set a timer — even if you don't finish, the pressure builds exam-day habits. Start with 15 minutes, work down to 10.
Do this last — after you've studied individual modules. Use Session Stats afterward to see which modules scored lowest.
After the exam, check Session Stats → Module Performance — any module under 60% goes back into your study queue.
Run it twice the night before an exam — different questions each time. Your second score should be meaningfully higher than your first.
Power Prompts — Copy & Use in Tutor Chat
Simplify
"Explain [concept] as if I'm hearing it for the first time — no jargon, just the core idea and why it matters."
Real World
"Give me 3 well-known companies that applied [framework] successfully and 2 that got it wrong — explain what made the difference."
Exam Prep
"Write me a challenging exam question on [concept], then tell me what a high-scoring answer would include without giving it away."
Compare
"What's the key difference between [concept A] and [concept B]? When would you use one over the other?"
Apply It
"How would a [specific company type] in [industry] apply [framework]? What would the biggest challenges be?"
Common Mistakes
"What are the most common mistakes MBA students make when analyzing [concept], and how do you avoid them?"
Go Deeper
"I think I understand [concept] — can you give me a scenario where the obvious answer is wrong and explain why?"
